Veterans Day (originally known as Armistice Day) is a federal holiday in the United States observed annually on November 11, for honoring military veterans of the United States Armed Forces. Unlike Memorial Day, which is an older holiday dating back to 1868 and specifically honors those who died in service, Veterans Day celebrates all U.S. military veterans, living and deceased. Unlike Memorial Day, Veterans Day pays tribute to all American veterans—living or dead—but especially gives thanks to living veterans who served their country honorably during war or peacetime.
